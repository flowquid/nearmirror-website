Window Settings

The Window tab controls how the mirror window appears on your desktop.

Window Title​

The mirror window is always titled NearMirror. This cannot be changed and helps you identify it among your open windows.

Position & Size​

Set the initial position and dimensions of the mirror window when it opens.

FieldDescription
XHorizontal position of the top-left corner (pixels from left edge of screen)
YVertical position of the top-left corner (pixels from top edge of screen)
WidthWindow width in pixels
HeightWindow height in pixels

Leave any field blank to let the window use its default size and position.

Borderless​

Removes the window title bar and borders. The mirror fills the window area edge to edge without any chrome. To move a borderless window, drag it from the mirrored content area.

Always on Top​

Keeps the mirror window above all other windows on your desktop, even when another app is focused. Useful when you want to monitor your device while working in another app.

Fullscreen​

Opens the mirror window in fullscreen mode. You can toggle fullscreen at any time with the MOD+F shortcut.

No Window​

Runs the mirroring session without opening a mirror window at all. The session is active in the background — audio still plays and recording still works — but there is no visual output on your desktop.

This is useful for background audio streaming or recording sessions where you don't need to see the screen.

Disable Screensaver​

Prevents your desktop screensaver from activating while a mirroring session is running. Useful for long sessions, presentations, or when running the device as a display.
